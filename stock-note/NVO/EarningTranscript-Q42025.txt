Novo Nordisk reported its fourth-quarter earnings for 2025, surpassing expectations with an earnings per share (EPS) of $1.02, compared to the forecasted $0.92. Revenue also exceeded predictions, reaching $12.53 billion against an expected $11.99 billion. Despite these positive results, Novo Nordisk’s stock fell 4.22% in pre-market trading, closing at $48.18, a 14.64% decline from the previous day’s close.

Key Takeaways
Novo Nordisk’s Q4 2025 EPS beat expectations by 10.87%.
Revenue for the quarter was 4.5% higher than anticipated.
The stock dropped 14.64% post-earnings, reflecting investor concerns.
Wegovy pill launch in the U.S. shows promising initial uptake.
Company faces pricing headwinds and market dynamics challenges.
Company Performance
Novo Nordisk showed robust performance in 2025, with a 10% increase in sales and a 6% growth in operating profit. The company remains a leader in the GLP-1 market, boasting a 62% market volume share. However, the gross margin decreased to 81% from 84.7% in 2024, indicating pressure on profitability.

Financial Highlights
Revenue: $12.53 billion, up 4.5% from forecast
Earnings per share: $1.02, exceeding forecast by 10.87%
Gross margin: 81%, down from 84.7% in 2024
Net profit: 102 billion Danish kroner
Earnings vs. Forecast
Novo Nordisk’s actual EPS of $1.02 surpassed the forecasted $0.92, marking a 10.87% surprise. The revenue of $12.53 billion also exceeded expectations by 4.5%. This performance reflects a strong quarter despite the challenges in the market.

Market Reaction
Despite the earnings beat, Novo Nordisk’s stock fell 4.22% in pre-market trading, closing at $48.18. This decline may be attributed to investor concerns over the company’s future guidance and pricing pressures. The stock’s performance is notable given its 52-week range of $43.08 to $93.8.

Outlook & Guidance
For 2026, Novo Nordisk anticipates adjusted sales growth between -5% and -13% at constant exchange rates, citing pricing headwinds and market dynamics as significant challenges. The company expects Medicare Part D coverage for obesity medicines by mid-2026, which could positively impact future sales.

Executive Commentary
CEO Mike Doustdar emphasized the strategic decision to reduce prices as an investment for future market capture: "Price reduction, in some ways, is our investment for the future and for capturing more patients." CFO Carsten Munk Knudsen highlighted the dynamic nature of the obesity market, stating, "The obesity market is just significantly more dynamic than most other markets."

Risks and Challenges
Pricing headwinds could impact profitability.
Market dynamics and competition in the GLP-1 segment.
Potential delays in Medicare Part D coverage for obesity medicines.
Ongoing restructuring costs amounting to approximately 8 billion Danish kroner.
Fluctuations in exchange rates affecting international sales.
Q&A
During the earnings call, analysts focused on pricing impacts and volume potential, the sustainability of demand for the Wegovy pill, and expectations for Medicare coverage. Executives addressed these concerns, emphasizing ongoing investment in research and development and market expansion efforts.
