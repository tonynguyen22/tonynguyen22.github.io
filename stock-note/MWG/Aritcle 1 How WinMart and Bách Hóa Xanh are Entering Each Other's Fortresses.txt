Key Summary of the Article
1. Financial Milestone
2025 marks the second consecutive year of profit for both chains.

WinCommerce (WinMart/Win): Reported a post-tax profit of approximately 501 billion VND, a massive 86.6x increase compared to 2024.

Bách Hóa Xanh: Reported a profit of approximately 710 billion VND, showing significant growth over 2024.

2. Opposite Expansion Strategies
Both chains are moving out of their "home turf" to capture new markets:

WinMart (Strong in the North): Is pushing into the South, specifically targeting rural areas to surround the cities. They use a "low capex" model that is easy to replicate.

Bách Hóa Xanh (Strong in the South): Is launching a major "Go North" campaign. About 30-40% of their 1,000 planned new stores this year will be located in Northern Vietnam.

3. Strategic Differences

WinMart: Utilizes a mix of "WinMart+" (mini-marts) and large supermarkets. They focus on existing store renovations and high-efficiency models (EBITDA margins around 9.3% in rural areas).

Bách Hóa Xanh: Focuses on a standardized "mini-mart" formula. They believe their fresh food offerings will be the key differentiator to win over Northern consumers who usually shop at traditional wet markets.

4. Market Outlook
Experts from Vietcap Securities note that modern retail (MT) currently accounts for less than 15% of the market in Vietnam. With 85% still held by traditional trade (GT), there is immense "empty land" for both companies to grow without direct, destructive confrontation. The goal for the industry is to reach a 20-25% market share by 2030.
